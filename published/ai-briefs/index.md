# AI Briefs

**AI Briefs** are short, plain-language reads of the AI work you do not have time to read in full. They are written for a decision-maker, not an academic. Each one covers the same three things: what the source says, how much to trust it, and what it means for you.

They come in two kinds, split by what is being briefed.

- **[Research Briefs](/ai-briefs/research-briefs/index.md)** cover notable AI research *papers* — what a study found, how strong the evidence is, and why it might matter to you. Sourced from the papers themselves.

- **[Industry Briefs](/ai-briefs/industry-briefs/index.md)** cover analyst and market *commentary* — an argument or thesis, the evidence behind it, and where the author's stake shapes the story.

The split matters because the two ask for different kinds of trust. A peer-reviewed finding and an analyst's opinion carry different weight, and each kind of brief is honest about which you are reading. Every brief links back to its original — a brief is our read of the work, not a replacement for it.
