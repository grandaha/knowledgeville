No enablement team is large enough to change how a whole organization works. Champions are how adoption scales past you: trusted peers inside each team who make AI normal by example. Built well, the network extends your reach. Left unsupported, it decays.

## When to use this

Stand up a champion network once you have a beachhead workflow and a sponsor. Champions multiply a working thing. They cannot rescue a tool nobody wants yet.

## Sourcing champions

The best champions are rarely the most senior people. Look for:

- Respected peers whose teammates already ask them for help.
- People already using AI on their own, including the shadow users.
- Enthusiasm paired with credibility. A champion nobody trusts adds nothing.

Recruit through managers, an open call, and the AI council. Aim for coverage of every team that matters, not a headcount target.

## What champions actually do

Define the role before you train for it. A champion:

- Answers "how would I do this with AI" for their team, in their own context.
- Shares working prompts and local wins.
- Feeds friction and unmet needs back to you.

A champion is a multiplier and a sensor. A champion is not a help desk, and not a manager of anyone.

## Training and recognition

Give champions more than a login.

- Train them on the beachhead workflows, and on how to coach, not only on the mechanics.
- Give them early access and a direct line to you.
- Recognize the work visibly. Manager awareness, time carved out, and public credit outlast a mug or a badge.

Recognition is the budget line most often forgotten. It is a common reason networks decay.

## Sustaining the network

A champion network is not a launch event. It is a standing commitment.

- Meet on a cadence. A community that never convenes stops being one.
- Refresh it. Champions move on, and coverage decays as teams change.
- Feed them new material, and close the loop on the friction they report.

Treat sustaining as equal work to starting. Most programs fund the launch and starve the upkeep.

## Watch out for

- **Volunteers without time.** A champion given no time to champion will stop.
- **Recognition theatre.** A title with no real support reads as an unpaid second job.
- **Coverage gaps.** A network that skips the skeptical teams leaves the hardest adoption untouched.
- **Set and forget.** An untended network stops working as one.
