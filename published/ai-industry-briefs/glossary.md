Plain-language definitions of the terms this bundle uses. It grows as the bundle does.

**Analyst brief**
A piece of commentary from an industry analyst or research firm that argues a thesis about
the market, usually backed by surveys or proprietary data. An analyst brief is an opinion
with evidence — not a peer-reviewed study. We treat the piece as a primary source for its
own figures.

**Primary source**
The original publication we're briefing — the analyst article, vendor report, or market
commentary itself. A brief is based on one primary source. If that source cites another,
we trace back to the original where we can.

**Stake / conflict of interest**
When an author or their employer has a financial or competitive interest in a conclusion.
Stake doesn't make a piece wrong — it's a signal to read more carefully. We note it in
the "How much to trust it" section of every brief.

**Pilot-to-production gap**
The tendency of enterprise AI initiatives to succeed in limited pilots but fail to scale
into production systems used by real employees day-to-day. A recurring theme in adoption
analysis.

**Services-as-Software**
The thesis, associated with HFS Research and others, that AI will shift the enterprise
services market from human-delivered work billed by the hour toward software-delivered
outcomes. Relevant to how adoption analysts model AI's long-term market impact.
