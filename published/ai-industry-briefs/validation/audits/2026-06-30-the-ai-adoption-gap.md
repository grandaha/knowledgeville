## Purpose

This record documents the citation sweep for the bundle's first brief,
[The AI Adoption Gap](/ai-industry-briefs/market-and-adoption/the-ai-adoption-gap.md). Under this
bundle's posture, the briefed analyst article is the **primary source for its own figures**, so the
sweep verifies that every figure on the page matches what the article states, and that each is
tagged with the right provenance.

**Audit conducted:** June 30, 2026.

**Scope:** the single page above, against its source, the HFS Research article *"The AI balloon is
bursting and services must be ready to pick up the pieces"* (Saurabh Gupta and Phil Fersht, June 25,
2026).

## Method

Every figure on the page was checked verbatim against the live article: the source header (title,
authors, date), the four public-market figures, the four adoption figures, the four forecast
figures, and the 90-day outcome bar. Each was classified by provenance — the author's own, attributed
to a third party, or publicly checkable — and the page's tags were checked against that
classification.

## Summary verdict

**Clean.** All fifteen figures match the article exactly. None was fabricated, misquoted, or
misattributed; the arithmetic complements (13% / 87%, 20% / 80%) are consistent. The page's
provenance tags are accurate: the load-bearing numbers are correctly marked as HFS's own, and the
public-market facts are correctly marked publicly checkable with the explicit caveat that the article
names no data provider.

The brief also discloses, in *How much to trust it* and *The fine print*, that HFS's own figures are
self-attributed and externally unverified, and that the public-market facts should be re-sourced to
filings before reuse. Those disclosures are accurate and are the point of the bundle's posture, not a
defect.

## Follow-ups

None. The page is sound as published.
