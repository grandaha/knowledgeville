Plain-language definitions of the terms this bundle uses. It grows as the bundle does.

**AI-native organization** — an organization whose structure was shaped around
human-plus-agent work from the start (or has fully arrived there through transformation),
as opposed to one still layering AI onto a structure built for an all-human workforce.

**Pod** — a small human team (typically a handful of people) that directs and is
accountable for a larger fleet of AI agents running an end-to-end process, replacing a
much larger all-human team doing the same work.

**Owner / Architect / Verifier** — the three accountability families this bundle argues
replace individual functional job titles as the organizing unit of work in an AI-native
organization. See [The Three Accountabilities](/ai-native-organization/the-three-accountabilities.md).

**Partnership pyramid** — the professional-services staffing model (few partners, many
leveraged associates) that this bundle argues generalizes to other organization types once
AI agents supply the leverage. See [The Partnership Pyramid, Generalized](/ai-native-organization/the-partnership-pyramid-generalized.md).
