# AI Research Briefs

New AI research lands every day, and almost none of it is written for you. The papers
are dense, the claims are easy to overstate, and you don't have an afternoon to spend
decoding one. This bundle does that part for you.

Each page here is a brief of a single research paper, written for someone who won't read
the original. You get what the paper found, an honest read on how much to trust it, and the
part that matters most: what you'd do differently because of it.

We don't try to cover everything. We pick papers that are getting real attention and
matter to people putting AI to work, then write the brief so you can decide in a few
minutes whether the full paper is worth your time.

## How it's organized

Briefs are grouped by theme, so you can follow the area you care about:

- **[Agents and Tool Use](/ai-research-briefs/agents-and-tool-use/index.md)** — AI that takes multi-step action and uses tools.
- **[Reasoning and Capabilities](/ai-research-briefs/reasoning-and-capabilities/index.md)** — what models can and can't do, and how they plan.
- **[Training and Efficiency](/ai-research-briefs/training-and-efficiency/index.md)** — how models are built, tuned, and made cheaper to run.
- **[Evaluation and Benchmarks](/ai-research-briefs/evaluation-and-benchmarks/index.md)** — how AI gets measured, tested, and compared.
- **[Safety and Alignment](/ai-research-briefs/safety-and-alignment/index.md)** — keeping AI useful and reducing real harms.

New to the bundle? Start with **[How These Briefs Work](/ai-research-briefs/how-these-briefs-work.md)** — it shows what's in every brief and how to read the "how much to trust it" section.
