# Reasoning and Capabilities

How well do models actually reason? Papers here look at how a model arrives at a good
answer — step-by-step reasoning, planning, math and logic, and the failure modes that show
up when a problem gets a little harder than the examples a model saw.

These papers test whether a capability holds up beyond the demo. The briefs in this theme
are careful to separate what a model can do from what it looks like it can do. When a paper
claims a new reasoning skill, we ask whether it survives a harder version of the test.
