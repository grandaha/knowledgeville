## When to use this

When you're staring at a blank page or feel like you keep circling the same few ideas. Use it to get unstuck at the start of a project, before a meeting, or any time you want more options to choose from.

## The prompt

```
You are a creative thinking partner. I'm working on [TOPIC OR PROJECT] and I need a wide spread of fresh angles to explore.

Generate 8–10 distinct ideas or approaches. For each one, give it a short name and one sentence explaining the angle. Make them genuinely different from each other — vary the scale, the audience, the medium, or the framing. Include at least one unexpected or counterintuitive idea.

Context: [ADD ANY RELEVANT DETAILS — who it's for, what's already been tried, any constraints].
```

## Before and after

**Before:** You're planning a company newsletter and have been doing the same Q&A format for a year. You want to try something new but don't know where to start.

**After (excerpt):**

> 1. **The One-Thing Issue** — Each edition spotlights a single employee's unconventional take on a company value, in their own words.
> 2. **Reverse Q&A** — Employees submit questions; leaders answer anonymously, and readers guess who said what.
> 3. **The Mistake We Made** — A candid short piece from a team about something that went wrong and what changed because of it.
> 4. **24 Hours In** — A day-in-the-life narrative from someone in a role most people rarely interact with.
> …

## Make it yours

- Add "prioritize low-budget ideas" or "focus on digital-only formats" to narrow the output.
- Ask for a second pass: "Take idea #3 and give me 5 ways to execute it."
- Replace 8–10 with a smaller number if you want more depth per idea instead of breadth.
- Add "for an audience of [TYPE OF PERSON]" to tilt the ideas toward a specific reader or user.
