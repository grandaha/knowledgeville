## When to use this

You've got a conversation coming up that makes you anxious — a tough performance review, a salary negotiation, telling a client bad news, or asking your landlord to fix something. Use this to run through it before the real thing.

## The prompt to start

```
I need to practice a difficult conversation. You'll play [WHO YOU'RE TALKING TO — e.g., "my manager", "a frustrated customer", "my landlord"]. Stay in character throughout. Here's the situation: [BRIEF CONTEXT — what's going on and what outcome you're hoping for].

Start the conversation as them. Be realistic — don't make it too easy. After we finish, step out of character and give me brief feedback on what went well and what I could do differently.
```

## How the back-and-forth goes

**You:** *(paste the prompt with your situation filled in)*

**AI:** *(as manager)* Hey, do you have a minute? I wanted to follow up on the project timeline. We're three weeks out from the deadline and the client is asking questions I don't have answers to.

**You:** Yeah, I've been meaning to loop you in. We hit a blocker with the data — the vendor didn't deliver what we expected. I should have flagged it sooner.

**AI:** *(as manager)* I appreciate you saying that. What's your plan to get back on track? And how confident are you in the new timeline?

**You:** I've drafted a revised schedule. I think we can still hit the client deadline if we bring in one more person for the next two weeks.

**AI:** *(as manager)* Okay. Who did you have in mind, and have you talked to them yet?

*(After a few more exchanges, you type "stop" or "let's debrief")*

**AI:** *(stepping out of character)* Good instinct to own the delay upfront — that set a collaborative tone. Your revised plan gave me something concrete to work with. One thing to tighten: when I asked how confident you were, you moved past it quickly. Managers want a number or a range, even a rough one. Try something like "70% confident, here's what would change that." <!-- noev: worked example -->

## Make it yours

- **Crank up the pressure:** Add "Be skeptical and push back — I want this to be harder than the real thing."
- **Replay a moment:** "Let's go back to where I asked about the budget. I want to try that differently."
- **Ask for a script:** After the debrief, ask "Can you give me two or three ways I could have phrased that better?"
- **Change the register:** "Play this as friendly but still firm" or "Make them frustrated from the start."

## Watch out for

AI won't react the way a real person will — it can't replicate someone's specific mood, history with you, or interpersonal quirks. Use this to get your words and structure right, not to predict exactly how the conversation will go. The real thing will surprise you; that's fine. This just means you show up less rattled.
