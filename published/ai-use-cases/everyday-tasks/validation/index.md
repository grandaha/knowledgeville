# Validation

The pages in this bundle are hands-on recipes — a ready-to-run prompt with a before-and-after example, not research essays. So most carry few or no citations, and that's deliberate. What we check here isn't statistical sourcing; it's accuracy in the things you'll actually act on.

The one place facts can quietly go wrong is embedded platform detail: character limits, what a specific tool can or can't do, a menu option that changed. Any claim of that kind is verified against the platform's own documentation before the page goes live. If the docs say something different from what the AI suggested, the page gets corrected — because a wrong factual claim undermines trust even on a quick how-to page.

When a validation sweep happens — whether a new-page review or a periodic freshness check — a dated record is added to the `audits/` directory here. That record notes what was checked, what (if anything) was corrected, and when.
