## When to use this

The update is written. What you can't script is the Q&A. A director asks why margins slipped; an investor wants the real story behind the churn number. Use this before a board meeting or investor call to surface the hard questions in advance and rehearse answers you actually believe.

This is not a one-shot prompt. It's a back-and-forth: you treat the AI as a skeptical board member, it presses, you answer, it presses harder. The goal is to find the questions that would catch you flat-footed while you still have time to think.

## A prompt to start

Open the loop with something like this, then keep the conversation going:

```
You are a sharp, skeptical board member preparing for my upcoming board meeting. I'll paste my update below. Your job is to find the weak spots.

First, list the 8–10 hardest questions a board or investor would ask after reading this — the ones I'd least want to get. Rank them by how uncomfortable they are. Then wait.

I'll answer them one at a time. After each answer, push back the way a real director would: probe what I dodged, ask for the number behind the claim, or point out where I'm being vague. Don't let me off easy. Don't accept a non-answer.

My update:
[PASTE YOUR BOARD UPDATE OR KEY POINTS]
```

## Work through it step by step

1. **Get the question list first.** Read the questions before you answer any. Some you'll have ready; the ones that make you wince are where the prep pays off.
2. **Answer out loud, then type.** Say your answer as you would in the room, then paste it in. You're rehearsing delivery, not drafting a memo.
3. **Let it push back.** When it challenges an answer, don't defend — fix. If it caught a dodge, your real director will too. Tighten the answer until the pushback stops landing.
4. **Hunt for the number behind each claim.** If you say growth is "strong," it should ask "compared to what?" Have the figure ready, and check it against the source.
5. **Find the one question you can't answer well.** There's usually one. That's the most valuable output of the session. Now you can prepare a real answer, line up the data, or decide how to handle it honestly in the room.

## What stays your call

The answers have to be *yours* — your real positions, your real commitments. The AI is good at generating questions and stress-testing phrasing. It has no idea what you can actually deliver next quarter, or what you're willing to promise a board. Use it to find the questions and pressure-test the wording. Never let it talk you into a commitment you don't mean, or a confident-sounding answer you can't stand behind when you're asked it for real.

## Watch out for

- **A smooth answer is not a true one.** AI will help you phrase almost anything persuasively, including things that aren't right. Rehearse honesty, not spin — a board remembers the answer that turned out to be wrong.
- **Keep sensitive specifics out of a tool you don't control.** Named personnel issues, unannounced deals, legal exposure — don't paste those into a chat tool. Rehearse those questions in general terms, or offline.
- **It will invent plausible board questions that don't fit your situation.** Use the list as a prompt for your own judgment, not a script. You know your board; drop the questions that don't apply and add the ones it missed.
