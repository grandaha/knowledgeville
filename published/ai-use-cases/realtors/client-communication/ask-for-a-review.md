## When to use this

You just closed a deal and your client is thrilled. That's the moment to ask — not weeks later when the feeling has worn off. But a generic "please leave me a review" rarely lands well. Use this to write a short, warm message that's easy to send and easy for them to act on.

It works for any closing: buyer, seller, rental, or referral.

## The prompt

Paste this into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. Fill in the brackets.

```text
You're helping me ask a satisfied client for an online review after closing.
Write a short, warm, personal message I can send by text or email.

Details:
- Client name: [first name or first names]
- One highlight of the deal: [e.g. "we found them a home in a tough market,"
  "I helped them sell above asking," "we closed in 21 days"]
- Where I'd like the review: [Google / Zillow / both]
- Tone: [friendly and warm / professional / casual]

Rules:
- Open with something personal — reference the highlight, not a generic
  "it was great working with you."
- Keep it under 80 words.
- Include ONE clear call to action with a placeholder for the direct review
  link: [YOUR GOOGLE REVIEW LINK] or [YOUR ZILLOW REVIEW LINK].
- Do NOT offer or imply any reward, discount, gift, or incentive for leaving
  a review.
- Do NOT ask them to only leave a positive review — the ask must be open
  to honest feedback.
- End on a warm, low-pressure note. No guilt, no urgency.
```

## Before and after

**What you start with** — your rough idea before the prompt:

> Hey! If you have a second, would you mind leaving me a Google review? It would really help me out. Thanks so much!

**What you get back:**

> Hi Sarah and James — finding you that corner unit in a market this tight was genuinely one of my favorite moments this year. If you're up for it, I'd love it if you shared your honest experience: [YOUR GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]. No pressure at all — I just wanted you to know how much this one meant to me.

The rewrite names a moment they'll actually remember, makes the ask feel personal, and leaves zero pressure.

## Make it yours

- **Send it while it's fresh.** The day of closing or the day after works best. Waiting a week cuts response rates in half.
- **Text over email.** A short text gets read and acted on faster. Save email for clients who prefer it.
- **Make the link one tap.** Go to your Google Business Profile or Zillow agent page, copy the direct review URL, and paste it in before you send. A link that takes two steps loses people.
- **One platform, not both.** Asking for reviews on two platforms in the same message feels like homework. Pick the one that matters most to your business right now.
- **Customize the highlight.** Swap in the exact detail that made this deal memorable. The more specific it is, the more it sounds like you.

## Watch out for

**Don't offer anything in exchange.** The FTC's Rule on Consumer Reviews and Testimonials (effective October 2024) prohibits offering payment, discounts, gifts, or any incentive in exchange for a review. Google's policy says the same: no "payment, discounts, free goods and/or services in exchange for posting any review." The prompt enforces this, but double-check any edits you make before you send.

**Don't screen clients before you ask.** Review gating means sending the ask only to clients you expect to be happy, while skipping the ones who had a rough experience. Google explicitly prohibits it: "discouraging or prohibiting negative reviews, or selectively soliciting positive reviews from customers." Ask everyone, or set a neutral policy — for example, every closed transaction. The prompt keeps the ask open to honest feedback, which is the right posture.

**Reviews must be genuine.** Both the FTC and Google require reviews to reflect real, firsthand experience. Don't coach clients on what to say, ask them to mention a specific detail, or suggest a rating. Let them write what they actually experienced.
