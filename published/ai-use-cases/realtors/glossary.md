Plain-language definitions of the terms these recipes use. It grows as the bundle does.

- **Fair housing** — the law that bars discrimination in housing. In practice, it means your
  copy describes the *home*, never the ideal buyer, and avoids language tied to race,
  religion, family status, disability, or other protected classes.
- **MLS** — the Multiple Listing Service, the shared database agents use to list and find
  properties. Each MLS has its own rules for what a listing can say.
- **FSBO** — "for sale by owner," a seller marketing a home without an agent.
- **Expired listing** — a listing whose contract ran out before the home sold. A common
  prospecting target.
- **Farming** — marketing consistently to one neighborhood or group to become the agent
  people there think of first.
- **Sphere of influence** — the people who already know you (past clients, friends, contacts)
  and are your warmest source of referrals.
- **Contingency** — a condition in an offer that must be met for the sale to go through, such
  as financing, inspection, or appraisal.
- **Demo-first** — how these pages are built: the value is a prompt you run, shown with a
  real before-and-after, not a cited essay.
