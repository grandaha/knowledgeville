Every guide in this bundle helps you make one decision well. It does not make the call for you. It gives you the options, the criteria that matter, the tradeoffs with evidence behind them, and the conditions under which each choice wins. Once you know the shape, you can read any guide in a couple of minutes and find the part you need.

## What's in every guide

The same sections, always in this order:

- **The decision** — what you're deciding, in a sentence, and who tends to face it.
- **Does this apply to you?** — when this decision comes up, so you know the guide fits your situation before you read on.
- **The options** — the real choices, each described honestly, without strawmen.
- **What actually matters** — the handful of criteria that should drive the call, not everything you could weigh.
- **How the options compare** — the options against those criteria, with the evidence and sources on the claims that carry weight.
- **When each one wins** — plain "if your situation is X, lean toward Y." This is the part to slow down on.
- **How to decide** — a short aid: the few questions to answer about your own situation, in order.
- **Watch out for** — the common traps, how reversible the choice is, and when to bring in a human expert.

## How to read one

Skim **The options** and **What actually matters** to frame the choice. Then spend your time in **When each one wins** and **How to decide** — that's where the guide meets your situation. The **How the options compare** section is where the sourcing lives, so it's the place to check a claim you want to lean on.

## Using a guide with your AI assistant

This is where a guide earns its keep. Connect this knowledge base to your AI assistant over [MCP](/open-knowledge-format/02-the-potential/01-knowledge-for-agents.md), then ask it to help you decide — "help me think through build vs. buy for our situation." Instead of the confident, unsourced list a generic chatbot produces, your assistant works from the guide. It walks you through the real criteria, weighs the sourced tradeoffs, and applies the "when each wins" logic to what you tell it. You still make the call — but on grounded, checkable ground rather than vibes.

## What a guide is not

A guide is not a recommendation for your specific situation. And it's not a substitute for advice when the stakes are high or the details are unusual. It gives you a well-framed, sourced way to think, dated to when it was written. When a lot rides on the call, use it to get sharp. Then check the load-bearing facts against their sources, and bring in someone who knows your specifics.
