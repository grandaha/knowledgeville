The technical components of data readiness can all be in place — quality monitoring automated, governance policies documented, access patterns modernized — and the program can still stall. The reason is almost always organizational: the data team owns the work but not the mandate, business stakeholders see data governance as IT overhead, and nobody with authority has made data behavior a measured expectation.

The **[Building a Data Culture](/enterprise-ai-transformation/tracks/03-data-readiness/strategy-and-organization/building-a-data-culture.md)** guide covers the organizational conditions that determine whether technical readiness programs hold. It is built around four evidence-backed cultural elements, the specific failure modes that derail most culture initiatives, and a five-level maturity model with an implementation sequence. The goal is not culture change as an abstract program — it is embedding data behavior into how the organization makes decisions and measures itself.
