Plain-language definitions of the terms this bundle uses. It grows as the bundle does.

**LLM Wiki** — Andrej Karpathy's pattern where a large language model incrementally builds
and maintains a persistent, interlinked markdown wiki from raw sources, instead of
re-deriving an answer from scratch on every query. See
[The Team Second Brain](/team-second-brain/the-team-second-brain.md).

**Single-player (single-user)** — built for one person's own account, machine, or chat
history. Contrast with a genuinely shared, multi-person knowledge base. Most tools built on
the LLM Wiki pattern are single-player today. See
[The Single-Player Ceiling](/team-second-brain/the-single-player-ceiling.md).

**Self-hosted** — software a team runs on its own server or machine rather than through a
vendor's hosted account. Usually free or low-cost, at the cost of someone on the team
setting it up and keeping it running.

**RAG (retrieval-augmented generation)** — the older approach an LLM Wiki replaces: search
raw documents fresh on every question, rather than maintaining a standing, curated wiki.
